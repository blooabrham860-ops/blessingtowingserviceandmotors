Frequently Asked Questions

1. How much does a tow truck service cost per mile?

The cost typically includes a base hook-up fee plus a mileage rate. For standard vehicles, the average is between $3 to $7 per mile, while heavy duty towing rates vary based on the equipment required.

2. Can I call for towing if I have a flat tire but no spare?

Absolutely. We provide roadside assistance flat tire services nationwide. If we cannot fix it on the spot, we will provide a flatbed tow truck to take you to the nearest tire center.

3. Does Blessing Towing Service offer 24/7 assistance?

Yes, we provide 24 hour towing near me services every day of the year. Whether it is a holiday or the middle of the night, our dispatchers are ready to help in the United States.

4. What is the difference between a wrecker and a flatbed?

A wrecker service typically uses a hook and chain or wheel-lift, while a flatbed towing service carries the entire vehicle on a platform. Flatbeds are often preferred for long-distance or luxury vehicle transport.

5. Do you provide semi truck roadside assistance for jump starts?

Yes, we offer semi truck roadside assistance including jump starts, pull-starts, and air line repairs to get your commercial vehicle moving without a full tow.

Eastpointe, MI Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

The community was first settled by Irish and German immigrants in the 1830s. In October 1897, a post office was established there with the name of "Half-way", as it was near the halfway point of the stage run between downtown Detroit and the Macomb County seat at Mount Clemens. It incorporated as the village of Halfway in December 1924 and reincorporated as the city of East Detroit in January 1929. Prior to 1924, most of the community formed a part of Erin Township. The city changed its name to "Eastpointe" after the change was approved by residents in a 1992 referendum; the name change had been proposed to remove any perceived association with the adjacent city of Detroit; the "pointe" suffix is intended to associate the city instead with the nearby affluent communities of the Grosse Pointes.
